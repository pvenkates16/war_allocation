1. War Regime: Core Principles

REGIME Long‑War Energy Context

  • Treat conflict as a structural condition
  • Expect repeated oil and shipping disruptions
  • Assume persistent energy‑driven inflation pressure
  • Favor energy‑resilient, high‑governance geographies
  • Avoid chokepoint‑dependent and ME‑exposed economies
  • Diversify across regions; no single‑country overweight
2. Portfolio Architecture (4 Buckets)

CORE Structural Energy‑Resilient Core (45–55%)

  • United States — energy independent, deep capital markets
  • Canada — hydro, nuclear, oil sands
  • Norway — hydro, oil, gas surplus
  • United Kingdom — North Sea, nuclear restart, grid build
  • Sweden & Finland — nuclear, hydro, biomass
  • Australia — coal, LNG, uranium, solar
  • Denmark, Netherlands — Nordic/EU grid integration
Rule: 55% if Brent > $120 for 6 months; 45% otherwise. No single country > 15% of bucket.

PRODUCERS Long‑Cycle Energy Producers (20–30%)

  • US shale & integrated majors
  • Canadian oil sands
  • Norwegian offshore (Equinor, Aker BP)
  • Brazilian deepwater (Petrobras), Guyana (offshore consortia)
  • Australian LNG (Woodside, Santos)
  • US LNG exporters (Cheniere, Venture Global)
Rule: 30% if ME supply disruption > 1 mb/d for 3 months; 20% otherwise.

INFRA Renewable & Grid Infrastructure (10–20%)

  • European offshore wind (UK, Germany, Netherlands, Denmark)
  • US grid modernization & IRA‑linked infra
  • EU interconnector & HVDC projects
  • Australian solar & transmission build‑out
  • Japan / Korea grid & storage
  • Global battery storage & transmission funds
Rule: 20% if global renewable CAPEX > 10% YoY; 10% otherwise.

DEFENSIVE Defensive Stability Assets (10–20%)

  • Short‑duration US Treasuries
  • German Bunds
  • Swiss franc & defensive equities
  • Gold & precious metals
  • Globally diversified utilities & infra funds
  • Singapore (financial centre, not energy)
Rule: 20% if VIX > 25 for 3 months; 10% otherwise.
3. Permanent Underweights

UNDERWEIGHT Structural Exclusions

  • High Middle‑East exposure: Turkey, Egypt, GCC importers
  • High oil‑import dependence: India, Japan equities (ex‑grid), Korea, Southern Europe
  • Shipping chokepoint exposure: Singapore trade flows, Malaysia, Suez‑dependent flows
  • Any single country > 20% of total portfolio
Rule: Keep exposure at 0–5% until the conflict regime ends.
